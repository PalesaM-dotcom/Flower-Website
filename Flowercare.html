Flower Care

Special Tips for Cut Flowers

  • Cut stems at an angle under water to prevent air bubbles.
  • Change water every 2–3 days and remove leaves submerged in water.
  • Add flower food or a teaspoon of sugar and a few drops of bleach to extend life.

Temperature & Humidity

  • Most flowers thrive in moderate temperatures: 18–25°C (65–77°F).
  • Indoor flowers like orchids and African violets enjoy higher humidity. Use a humidity tray or mist lightly.

Water & Maintenance

  • Change water every 2–3 days and re-trim stems each time.
  • Remove wilted flowers or leaves promptly.
  • Refill water as it drops to keep stems submerged.

Placement

  • Keep the vase in a cool area, away from direct sunlight, heaters, or drafts.

Optional Tips to Extend Life

  • Adding a few drops of bleach or a penny to the vase can reduce bacteria.
  • For flowers like roses, adding a pinch of sugar helps them stay fresh longer.
  • Some flowers, like tulips and daffodils, grow even in the vase, so make sure the vase is tall enough.
  • Avoid placing near fruits, especially bananas and apples—they release ethylene gas, which makes flowers age faster.
